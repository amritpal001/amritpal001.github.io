Arts and Crafts on Environmental Awareness

This workshop was made because it teaches us how to create plant pots on recyclable materials and it also aims to preserve/maintain a good environment.

Find Out More
Why is this workshop important?

Encouraging the participants and other professionals to engage to implement environmental awareness through the use of arts and crafts. It is important because it teaches us how to create plant pots on recyclable materials and it also aims to preserve/maintain a good environment.

Today we are facing environmental issues like pollution; there’s pollution on air, land masses, and water surfaces. According to a study, garbage dumped in the ocean is roughly at 14 billion pounds and the most major constituent is plastics. Imagine those marine lifes and dolphins! It’s truly saddening. We make 300 million of plastics a year and only just 9% of it is recycled since it’s thrown anywhere and everywhere! Instead of throwing away plastics after it’s used, why not put it into good use?

This is where our group came up with turning recyclable plastics/materials into plant pots to spread environmental awareness. When our workshop is spread, people start to wonder why we use plastics to put plants on and not just buy those ordinary plant pots? They will get curious and search for why, then they will come up with the answer ‘because our environment is worsening and we need to contribute to help the environment since we are one of the factors this is happening’. We can help not just the ocean and the marine lifes, even the plants to spread green to deforest lands and we can also lessen the production of plastics by factories and lessen pollution in the air.

Who should attend this workshop?


  • People that are:

    Studying environmental education, People who love plants, planting, and nature, and random people who want to help the environment in an easy and basic way.



  • People that are:

    Looking for something to do or activities to do while in quarantine and they can develop a good habit by our workshop.



  • What participants learn during the workshop?

    They will learn about how to create plant pots with the use of recyclable material, they will understand how important maintaining and preserving a good environment is, and they will learn how to save the environment by these simple acts of contributing and joining our workshop.

Usability Test Results:

Summary (I)

Our usability test was taken particularly during our dry run of the workshop to give them more idea of the concept. It took place on a Gmeet call that our team provided. We used Google forms where they put their own understanding of the workshop with the help of the guide questions enlisted. It was taken during the two dry runs that happened in a different date which is on November 30 and December 3.

Goals (II)

The goal is to look more into further improvements that should be implemented for the workshop. Participants take our test not to test them but to test the workshop in hand.

Methodology (III)

The questions are about the workshop itself. We use google forms in conducting our usability test and gather their answers and feedbacks. The participants have an experience in arts and crafts and that will help us in our workshop because they have knowledge in arts and crafts. We think that this informations we gathered will benefit our workshop and improve ourselves.

Participants (IV)

The questions are about the feedbacks of our workshop and the workshop itself. Most of the participants answered no need to improve or it’s good to go but this one participant which will give the name dave, gave a concrete feedback and opinion about our workshop. This really helps us know what to improve on in our workshop and possibly add the suggestions he gave. This will really benefit us in our workshop in the future. All of our participants are students and also love the idea of our workshop. And their answers are what we are looking for in our target audience but we hope that they also said the things what we need to improve in our workshop so it will help us create more ideas and add it in our workshop.

Findings (V)

1.) 100% of the participants said the workshop is Interesting, 66% for Helpful, 50% for Accurate,Likable and Reliability.

2.) While some do not have a say to the workshop, 1 participant openly said an informative observation from their point of view. By the suggestion of the participant we can have a more improved way of handling the workshop.

3.) 2 participants said gathering materials is the hardest part, 3 said designing/creating colors, 2 said the same thing which is the work itself, while 1 said she doesn't have any background knowledge of arts.
