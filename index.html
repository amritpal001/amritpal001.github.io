Arts and Crafts on Environmental Awareness

This workshop was made because it teaches us how to create plant pots on recyclable materials and it also aims to preserve/maintain a good environment.

Find Out More
Why is this workshop important?

Encouraging the participants and other professionals to engage to implement environmental awareness through the use of arts and crafts. It is important because it teaches us how to create plant pots on recyclable materials and it also aims to preserve/maintain a good environment.

Today we are facing environmental issues like pollution; there’s pollution on air, land masses, and water surfaces. According to a study, garbage dumped in the ocean is roughly at 14 billion pounds and the most major constituent is plastics. Imagine those marine lifes and dolphins! It’s truly saddening. We make 300 million of plastics a year and only just 9% of it is recycled since it’s thrown anywhere and everywhere! Instead of throwing away plastics after it’s used, why not put it into good use?

This is where our group came up with turning recyclable plastics/materials into plant pots to spread environmental awareness. When our workshop is spread, people start to wonder why we use plastics to put plants on and not just buy those ordinary plant pots? They will get curious and search for why, then they will come up with the answer ‘because our environment is worsening and we need to contribute to help the environment since we are one of the factors this is happening’. We can help not just the ocean and the marine lifes, even the plants to spread green to deforest lands and we can also lessen the production of plastics by factories and lessen pollution in the air.

Who should attend this workshop?


  • People that are:

    Studying environmental education, People who love plants, planting, and nature, and random people who want to help the environment in an easy and basic way.



  • People that are:

    Looking for something to do or activities to do while in quarantine and they can develop a good habit by our workshop.



  • What participants learn during the workshop?

    They will learn about how to create plant pots with the use of recyclable material, they will understand how important maintaining and preserving a good environment is, and they will learn how to save the environment by these simple acts of contributing and joining our workshop.

Facts About Our Workshop:

First (I)

Did you know: Since our workshop shows you how to make plants with pots, anyone can do it. Plus, it doesn't take much space in your house, so it can be stored anywhere as long as there's sunlight and water around the plant.

Second (II)

Did you know: Plants are really important for the planet and for all living things. Plants absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen from their leaves, which humans and other animals need to breathe. Living things need plants to live - they eat them and live in them. Plants help to clean water too.

Third (III)

Did you know: Pots can be turned into any shape, as long as soil can be stored in them, and water can go through. Plants can still grow normally in them.

Fourth (IV)

Did you know: Arts and crafts boosts self confidence and strongly practicing this correlates with higher achievement in reading and maths.

Fifth (V)

Did you know: Art cannot be taught, it comes from thinking. But it can be improved by practice.
